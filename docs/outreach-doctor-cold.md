# Cold-outreach scripts for recruiting pilot doctors

Goal: get **one** paying doctor on the ambient scribe for two weeks. That one doctor unlocks the testimonial slot on the homepage and turns the marketing site from "credible" to "battle-tested."

These scripts are written to convert. Use them verbatim or adapt to your voice — the structure (hook, proof, ask, friction) matters more than the exact words.

---

## Targeting

Best prospects, in order:

1. **Solo GPs in tier-1 cities** doing 25–60 patients/day. Pain is highest, technical-comfort is highest, decision is theirs alone.
2. **Paediatricians at 1–3 doctor clinics**. Long histories, parent-questions afterwards (post-visit Q&A is a clean wedge), notes are pure boilerplate after a while.
3. **Gynaecologists / dermatologists** in metro tier-1. High note volume, image-heavy charts, willing to pay for good tools.
4. **Psychiatrists.** Long sessions, dense narrative notes — ambient scribe is night-and-day for them. Smaller market but high willingness-to-pay.

Avoid in pilot phase:
- Hospital-employed doctors (procurement is brutal; you'll never close)
- Surgeons / interventional specialists (consultation share is too small to pitch on)
- Anyone over 60 (technology adoption curve)

Source list:
- Practo / Lybrate / 1mg directories — public GP profiles by area
- LinkedIn search "GP Mumbai" / "Family Physician Bangalore"
- Local clinic Google Business listings — owner is often the doctor
- Friend-of-friend warm intros (10x conversion vs cold)

## Follow-up cadence (don't send more than 4 total)

- **Day 0**: cold email (above)
- **Day 4**: short follow-up — *"Bumping this up — happy to send a 60-second video demo if a call is too much. Two questions and you decide."*
- **Day 11**: send the homepage demo link with a one-liner — *"This is what it looks like in action. Two minutes."*
- **Day 25**: final note — *"Closing the pilot list this week. Last chance if you want in. No reply means I'll stop following up."*

---

## What to do after they say yes

1. Schedule a 30-min onboarding call. You drive the screen, they watch.
2. Create their account through `/admin/applications` → approve → they get the welcome email with temp password.
3. Walk them through:
   - Adding 1-2 test patients
   - Recording a fake "ambient scribe" with their own voice
   - Looking at the AI patient summary
4. Hand off your phone number. Tell them to message you when something's broken or weird.
5. Day 5: check in — *"Anything weird? Anything saving you real time yet?"*
6. Day 14: ask for the testimonial. Send `docs/marketing-video-scripts.md` testimonial brief.

---

## What to do when they say no

Reply once, briefly:

> No worries — appreciate the look. If anything changes (or if you know a colleague this might fit), here's the homepage: https://www.odudoc.com. Best, [Name].

Don't argue. Don't pitch again. Doctors talk to each other.
