## OduDoc launches free clinic-management EMR for Indian doctors, with telemedicine and voice prescription in 11 Indian languages

**MUMBAI / [Date]** — OduDoc, an Indian telemedicine and electronic medical-records platform, launched today with a clinic-management product that is free for the first 50 patients per month — with no hidden fees, no per-seat subscription, and no time limit. Indian doctors and small clinics can sign up at https://www.odudoc.com/for-doctors and start consulting and managing patient records the same day.

Beyond 50 new patients in a single month, doctors pay a flat $50 (about ₹4,200) to lift the cap to 250 for the rest of that month. There is no auto-renewal — clinics that have a quieter month don't pay anything.

"Indian solo doctors and small clinics pay disproportionately for software they barely use," said [Founder name], founder of OduDoc. "An average two-doctor clinic spends ₹3,000–₹5,000 per month on EMR licences, ₹500–₹1,500 on appointment software, plus integration costs to make them talk to each other — most of which goes to features the clinic uses for fifteen minutes a day. We reversed the model: free until you have meaningful volume, then a flat $50 instead of per-seat subscription bloat."

### What's included on the free tier

- **Clinic EMR** — patients, SOAP visit notes, lab reports, scans, invoices, medical certificates with public verification URLs
- **23 prescription templates** with the doctor's own clinic letterhead
- **AI prescription assistant** with drug-interaction red flags
- **Voice prescription** in 90+ languages, including 11 Indian languages: Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, Marathi, Gujarati, Kannada, Malayalam, Punjabi, Urdu, Bengali (Bangladesh dialect)
- **Patient online-payment portal** — doctors share an auto-generated payment link, patients pay via Stripe Checkout, the invoice marks itself paid
- **Multi-staff team** — 1 nurse + 1 front-desk seat with role-based permissions on the free tier; 3 + 3 + 3 staff doctors on the $50 unlock
- **FHIR R4 + HL7 v2.5.1 export** — one-click migration to any other EMR, ever
- **Cross-border video / chat consultations** with 70% commission to the doctor and weekly Stripe payouts
- **GDPR Article 15 / 17 / 20 endpoints** built in for patient data export and erasure

### Built for India specifically

OduDoc auto-restricts India-licensed doctors to Indian patients per the Indian Medical Council's 2020 Telemedicine Practice Guidelines — the first telemedicine platform of its size to ship that compliance check by default rather than as a configuration option.

The platform also includes ABHA Health ID linking and Healthcare Professionals Registry (HPR) verification scaffolding, with full Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM) Phase 1 integration live on launch day. ABDM Phase 2 — real NHA OAuth and registry verification — is expected once OduDoc's sandbox application clears.

### Hospitality with healthcare-grade compliance

OduDoc's EMR ships with HIPAA-aligned audit trails (every patient-record access logged with timestamp + actor), GDPR data-protection endpoints, and IMC telemedicine compliance. A separate `/corporate` plan with BAA / DPA agreements, multi-clinic admin, and unlimited everything is available for hospitals and clinic networks.

## How to pitch this

**Subject lines that get opened by Indian healthtech journalists:**

- "Indian healthtech launch: free EMR for solo doctors, $50 cap unlock instead of subscription"
- "Voice prescription in 11 Indian languages — OduDoc launches today"
- "ABDM-ready telemedicine + EMR launches with IMC compliance built in"
- "[Founder] launches OduDoc — free clinic software for the first 50 patients/month"

**Pitch flow per outlet:**

1. **First email** — short version (200 words from the launch-announcement.md doc), the press-release attached as PDF, link to live site
2. **3 days later if no response** — follow-up with one specific data point ("we have N doctors signed up in the first 72 hours") or one specific quote
3. **Don't follow up more than twice** — Indian healthtech journalists are spammed; persistence past two emails gets you blacklisted

**Outlets ranked by likely response:**

1. MedicalDialogues — best for clinical-software stories
2. ET Health — broad reach but slow
3. YourStory Healthcare — startup-friendly, fast
4. Healthcare Executive — B2B angle (clinic-owner readers)
5. The Hindu BusinessLine Health — mainstream but harder
6. Mint Healthcare — finance-tilted angle works ($50 / month)

**Don't pitch:**
- TechCrunch / Forbes India unless you have a funding announcement
- Generic tech press until you have user numbers worth reporting
